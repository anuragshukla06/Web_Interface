About Us

We Are students of 4th semester, CSE branch , Dr.SPM International Institute of Information Technology, Naya Raipur. As a part of our Mini Project under Dr.Venkanna U. , we have designed an Automated Centralised Controlling and Monitoring System (Smart Warehouse) for ensuring optimal storage of Fruits inside the Warehouse.

OUR PROJECT

IDEA

A fruit warehouse is a place where fresh fruits are stored efficiently at specific temperatures, humidity and other parameters that help in preserving the fruits without over-ripening them. Internet of Things (IoT) is an ecosystem of connected physical objects that are accessible through the internet.  We would be able to track and count everything, and greatly reduce   waste, loss and cost.  We would know when things need replacing, repairing or recalling, and  whether they are fresh or past their best.

MOTIVATION

  • About 18% of fruits and vegetables are wasted every year in India due to lack of warehouses.
  • Traditional monitoring and control systems are completely manual hence are erroneous.
  • The absence of proper monitoring, controlling and warning systems leads to degradation in quality of fruits.
  • Consequently, we need an automated and centralized monitoring and control system to ensure optimal storage of fruits.

PROBLEMS IN EXISTING WAREHOUSES

  • Manual control of parameters like temperature, humidity values.
  • No centralised monitoring of environment variables.
  • Maintaining track of freshness level of seasonal fruits.
  • No provision for global control.

PROPOSED SOLUTION

  • Each sensor node consists of temperature, humidity and light sensors along with a microcontroller.
  • Microcontroller receives respective values from each of the sensors and sends it to cloud storage.
  • Many such sensor nodes are deployed in the warehouse at various positions to record and send the data.
  • Data received at the cloud storage is analysed on a central monitoring node which then sends the optimal values of those parameters to the environment controlling system.
  • The central monitoring node also regulates emergency signals along with providing global monitoring and control of the above parameters.

BACKGROUND PROCESSES

  • Sensors record environment parameters in real time.
  • Send them to the microcontroller.
  • Central monitoring node fetches the data from the cloud, makes it visualizable and display it on the website.
  • Monitoring node also sends the optimum environment parameter values to the temperature and humidity controlling system of the warehouse.
  • In our prototype, CPU fan maintains the temperature values provided by the monitoring node, analogous to already existing refrigeration systems in warehouses.
